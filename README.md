# [=-=-=-=-=-= Death Swap But Way Better =-=-=-=-=-=]

[ Dit spel ondersteunt Nederlands spelen / This map is available in Dutch! ]

Tired of the same old boring Minecraft Death Swap? We get it. That's why we decided to make Minecraft Death Swap... *Way Better*.

As usual, every few minutes, players swap positions with each other, and the idea is to build traps that'll take out your opponents right as you swap! Sounds like normal Death Swap, right?

Periodically, every 45 seconds, receive a choice of 3 different ridiculously powerful items, some of which may help you, while others will harm your opponents. Choose one of the three items by dropping it from your hotbar, and then just sit back and watch as the most chaotic Death Swap you've ever played unfolds right before you. Some items include spawning Oppenheimer's bomb, spawning the Warden, and directly teleporting someone to the Nether, amongst an additional 100+ items!

Play with a pre-determined number of lives (adjustable in the main lobby) and the last person to lose all their lives wins the game! There are also plenty of other customizable settings, like random swap cycle times!

> Settings: Minecraft version 26.1 vanilla; Supports 1-12 players

> A [Fabric server](https://fabricmc.net/use/server/) with performance mods is recommended due to the lag from world generation and certain items. For example, [Lithium](https://modrinth.com/mod/lithium), [C2ME](https://modrinth.com/mod/c2me-fabric), [FerriteCore](https://modrinth.com/mod/ferrite-core) (and [Fabric API](https://modrinth.com/mod/fabric-api))

> Usage: Place the `data`, `datapacks`, `dimensions`, `generated`, `players`, `level.dat`, `level.dat_old`, and `session.lock` folders/files into your server's `world` folder.

> Melumi11's preferred settings: enable hunger, start with nothing, show advancements, enable PVP, random swap cycles, show swap timer, 3 lives, swap warning: 1 minute

> Note: The datapack works by randomly warping players millions of blocks away. This causes world generation lag, large world sizes, and leftover blocks from previous games (most noticable in the end). Therefore, I recommend using performance mods and the (optional) manual `clear_world.sh` script periodically.
